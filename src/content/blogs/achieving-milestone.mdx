# The Roadmap: Mastering AI, ML, and Automating Everything I Can

I'll be honest. The reason I picked AI/ML wasn't just because it sounds impressive at family dinners. It's because I genuinely believe it's the closest thing we have to a superpower right now — and I want to use it.

## Where I Am Right Now

I'm currently pursuing B. Tech in Computer Science & Engineering (AI/ML) at Dr. D Y Patil University, RAIT. Every day I'm going deeper into the fundamentals — from data structures to neural networks, from Python scripts to full pipelines. I've already built things like an Adaptive AI-Powered Mock Interview System and a Sensitive Content Detection model, which honestly surprised even me.

But the journey is far from over. In fact, it's just getting interesting.

### Why AI and ML Specifically?

Because the gap between "what is currently done manually" and "what a well-trained model can do" is shrinking every single day. I want to be on the right side of that gap — the building side, not the watching side.

## The Real Plan: Automate Everything Possible

Here's where it gets exciting. My goal isn't just to understand AI — it's to **use** it aggressively across real-world fields:

- **Content Creation**: Use LLMs and generative tools to speed up writing, ideation, and research.
- **Video & Photo Editing**: Automate repetitive editing workflows — cuts, captions, colour grading — using AI pipelines.
- **Productivity & Workflows**: Build personal automation tools that handle the boring 20% so I can focus on the creative 80%.
- **AI Agents**: Eventually, systems that run tasks end-to-end with minimal human input.

The vision is simple: let the machines handle the grunt work, while I focus on building smarter machines.

## What to Expect From This Blog?

- **Project Breakdowns**: How I built my AI projects and what I learned (and broke) along the way.
- **Automation Ideas**: Practical experiments in automating creative and technical workflows.
- **Honest Progress Updates**: No fluff. Just what's working, what's not, and what's next.

## Conclusion

This is just the beginning. If you're into AI, automation, or just watching someone try to build cool things — stick around. There's a lot more coming. 🚀
